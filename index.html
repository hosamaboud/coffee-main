Our Story

From Ethiopia, coffee made its way to Yemen, where it was cultivated and brewed by the locals. Yemenis were the first to boil the beans, creating a rich, aromatic drink that quickly became part of their daily lives and rituals. Coffee was particularly cherished by Sufi monks, who drank it to stay awake during their late-night prayers and meditations. Over time, coffee's popularity spread across the Arab world, and coffee houses, known as qahveh khaneh, became gathering places for conversation, music, and culture.

Our Products

Espresso

Strong coffee made by forcing hot water through finely ground coffee beans.

Cappuccino

A mix of espresso, steamed milk, and milk foam, often topped with cocoa powder.

Latte

Similar to cappuccino but with more steamed milk and a little milk foam.

Mocha

A combination of latte and hot chocolate, with espresso, milk, and chocolate.

Americano

Espresso diluted with hot water, giving it a lighter taste.

Macchiato

Espresso topped with a small amount of steamed milk or foam.
